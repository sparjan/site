NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory




The Deep Space Network (DSN) is the communications network used by spacecraft to receive commands and relay data downlink. Scheduling when to do so is a complex process that results in high latency as missions wait on one another to respond to proposed equipment and time changes. At JPL, I worked with Dr. Mark Johnstonâ€™s team on the Robo Scheduling Services (ROBOSS) project to automate the response process for smaller missions and reduce proposal latency by predicting whether a mission is likely to concur or reject to a proposed change. I analyzed 75 weeks of change history data spanning 40 missions and implemented KNN, SGDC, Random Forest, and Decision Tree classifiers. MIT AI Policy for the World Project




I'm passionate about making sure the future of technology is designed to be inclusive of diverse stakeholders. As a research assistant with the AIPW, I work with former Mexican Secretary of Foreign Affairs, Dr. Luis Videgaray Caso, and MIT's Internet Policy Research Initiative to study the implementation and implications of systems designed to connect governance and policymaking to AI. So far, I've structured reports on AI and the UN Sustainable Development Goals, data aggregation and COVID-19 contact tracing, and the Chinese Social Credit System to advise policymakers interested in artificial intelligence technologies.
