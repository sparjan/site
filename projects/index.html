NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory

The Deep Space Network (DSN) is the communications network used by spacecraft to receive commands and relay data downlink. Scheduling when to do so is a complex process that results in high latency as missions wait on one another to respond to proposed equipment and time changes. At JPL, I worked with Dr. Mark Johnstonâ€™s team on the Robo Scheduling Services (ROBOSS) project to automate the response process for smaller missions and reduce proposal latency by predicting whether a mission is likely to concur or reject to a proposed change. I analyzed 75 weeks of change history data spanning 40 missions and implemented KNN, SGDC, Random Forest, and Decision Tree classifiers.
